Instructions for Use

  1. Print the icon pages on card stock or heavy paper if possible. Regular paper works too, but may not be as durable.
  2. Cut out the icons along the dashed lines, using the corner marks as guides.
  3. Optional: Laminate the icons for increased durability.
  4. Add to your visual schedule by placing them in the "Activity" or "Options" column as needed.
  5. For activities with multiple versions (walk, hike, catch), you can let Zach choose which version he prefers.

Tips for Using the Schedule

  • Provide structured choices - Use the "Options" column to give Zach choices within a framework that works for him.
  • Be consistent with using the schedule to help build predictable routines.
  • Set up the schedule together at the start of the day.
  • Balance activities - Include a mix of preferred and non-preferred activities.

  • Celebrate transitions - Acknowledge successful transitions between activities.

These icons have been specifically designed with Zach in mind:

  • Forest scenes for hiking (rather than mountains) to avoid height concerns
  • Consistent visual style across all icons
  • Clear, simple imagery with limited visual distractions
  • Bold text labels that are easy to read
  • Family-based activities showing father-son interactions
  • All icons sized to fit perfectly in the visual schedule template
