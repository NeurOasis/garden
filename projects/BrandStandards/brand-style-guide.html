📊Brand Overview

Your brand represents a balance of focus, clarity, and organization with energy and creativity. Designed to be accessible and visually clear, it provides consistent structure while remaining flexible enough to adapt to different contexts.

Core Values

  • Clarity: Making complex information accessible and understandable
  • Focus: Helping people direct their attention effectively
  • Flexibility: Adapting to different needs and energy levels
  • Structure: Creating organization without rigidity
  • Authenticity: Embracing natural thinking patterns

Brand Personality

  • Approachable: Warm and welcoming, never intimidating
  • Thoughtful: Deliberate and considered in all communications
  • Supportive: Encouraging and helpful, providing scaffolding
  • Clear: Direct and transparent, avoiding ambiguity
  • Adaptable: Responsive to different contexts and needs

Color Usage

Use colors purposefully to create visual hierarchy and guide attention:

  • Deep Blue: Primary brand color, use for headers, important UI elements, and calls-to-action
  • Energetic Amber: Highlight color for priorities, buttons, and important alerts
  • Soft Sage: Success states, completion indicators, and secondary confirmations
  • Warm Coral: Notification states, important reminders, and secondary buttons
  • Lavender: Use for "waiting" states or items dependent on others
  • Mint: Project-related elements and organizational tools

Color Contrast

Always ensure there is sufficient contrast between text and background colors, with a minimum ratio of 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text.

🔤Typography

The typography system is designed for maximum readability and clear hierarchy, with a focus on comfortable reading across devices. System fonts are used for their universal availability and consistency.

Body Text (1rem) - The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog. This is an example of body text, which should be easy to read and have sufficient line height.

Small Text (0.85rem) - Used for captions, footnotes, and secondary information.

System fonts provide excellent readability across devices and eliminate the need for additional font loading, improving performance.

Typography Guidelines

  • Line Length: Aim for 50-75 characters per line for optimal readability
  • Line Height: Body text should have a line height of 1.6 for comfortable reading
  • Font Weight: Use normal (400) for body text and bold (700) for headings
  • Font Size: Minimum 16px for body text, scale headings appropriately
  • Hierarchy: Maintain clear distinction between heading levels and body text
DO
  • Use bold for emphasis on important text
  • Maintain consistent heading hierarchy (H1 > H2 > H3)
  • Ensure sufficient contrast between text and background
  • Use ordered lists for sequential information
  • Include adequate whitespace between paragraphs
DON'T
  • Use all caps for entire paragraphs
  • Use more than two font families in a single design
  • Create text blocks wider than 75 characters
  • Use font sizes smaller than 16px for body text
  • Skip heading levels (e.g., H1 to H3 without H2)
